## Typed Notes vs Handwritten Notes

Typed notes are faster to create and edit. You can copy, paste, rearrange, and fix mistakes quickly.

Handwritten notes feel more visual and personal. They can be easier to scan when printed on paper.

A handwritten notes generator connects both workflows. You type first for speed, then convert for the handwritten look.

Before converting, remove unnecessary text. Keep only the important definitions, formulas, examples, and explanations.

Use clear headings for each topic. Headings make handwritten notes easier to scan.

### Step 3: Keep Paragraphs Short

## Best Settings for Handwritten Notes

### Use Lined Paper

Lined paper keeps notes structured and easy to follow.

### Use Blue or Black Ink

Blue and black ink look natural for student notes.

### Keep Font Size Medium

Small text may fit more content, but it becomes harder to read. Medium size is usually best.

### Use Natural Line Spacing

Line spacing should be comfortable. If lines are too close, the page looks packed.

### Preview Before Downloading

Always preview the page before export. Small spacing changes can improve the final result.
