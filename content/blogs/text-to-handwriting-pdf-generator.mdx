## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Making Text Too Small

Tiny handwriting may fit more words, but it becomes hard to read. Use a natural size.

### Using Fancy Fonts

Fancy cursive may look attractive, but it can feel fake for study pages. Choose a readable style.

### Ignoring Margins

Margins make handwritten pages look organized. Without margins, the page can look crowded.

### Downloading Before Previewing

Always preview before export. A few spacing changes can make the PDF look much better.

### Exporting Dense Text Without Breaks

Long blocks of text can make a handwritten PDF look fake and hard to read. Add headings, shorter paragraphs, and line breaks before converting.

## PDF vs PNG vs JPG

| Format | Best For | Avoid When |
| --- | --- | --- |
| PDF | Printing, multi-page documents, stable page layout | You need a single web image |
| PNG | Sharp preview images and screenshots | The page count is high |
| JPG | Smaller image files | You need crisp text for printing |

For most handwritten-style documents, start with PDF. It keeps the paper size, margins, and page order consistent.
